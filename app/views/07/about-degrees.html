About degrees

A degree is a higher education qualification that usually takes 3 or more years to complete if you study full time.

Types of degrees

There are different types of degrees you can take, including:

  • Bachelor's degree – the most common type, such as a BA, BSc or BEng
  • Integrated master's degree – combines a bachelor's and master's in one course
  • Foundation degree – a 2-year course that can lead to a full degree
  • Top-up degree – lets you turn a foundation degree into a full bachelor's degree

Subjects you can study

You can study a wide range of subjects at degree level. These include:

  • arts and humanities – such as English, history or philosophy
  • sciences – such as biology, chemistry or physics
  • social sciences – such as psychology, sociology or economics
  • professional subjects – such as law, medicine or teaching
  • technical subjects – such as engineering, computing or architecture

Entry requirements

Each university sets its own entry requirements. You’ll usually need A levels, T Levels, or equivalent level 3 qualifications.

Some courses ask for specific subjects or grades. You should check the university’s website for full details.

What you'll get

You’ll get a level 6 qualification when you complete a bachelor's degree. Find out what qualification levels mean .

How long it takes

A full-time degree usually takes 3 years to complete. Some degrees, such as medicine or architecture, can take longer.

You can also study part time, which takes longer but may suit your circumstances better.

Costs and financial support

Universities charge tuition fees for degree courses. You can apply for student finance to cover the cost.

You may also be able to get extra support through grants, bursaries or scholarships, depending on your personal situation or the subject you’re studying.
