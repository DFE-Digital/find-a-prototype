About BTECs

BTECs are vocational qualifications that focus on practical, hands-on learning. They can prepare you for work, an apprenticeship or further study, including university.

Types of BTECs

BTECs are available at different levels. The most common are:

  • BTEC Level 1 and 2 – equivalent to GCSEs
  • BTEC Level 3 – equivalent to A levels

Some BTECs combine classroom learning with work experience. You’ll complete assignments, projects and practical tasks instead of taking exams.

Subjects you can study

BTECs are offered in a wide range of subjects, including:

  • health and social care
  • business
  • engineering
  • IT and digital technology
  • sport and fitness
  • performing arts
  • construction and building services

Entry requirements

Entry requirements depend on the level of BTEC you want to take. For example:

  • Level 1 – may not require any formal qualifications
  • Level 2 – usually requires some GCSEs at grade 3 or above
  • Level 3 – often requires 4 or more GCSEs at grade 4 or above

You should check the specific course requirements on the college or provider’s website.

What you'll get

You’ll get a recognised qualification at the end of your BTEC. The level depends on the course you take. Find out what qualification levels mean .

BTEC Level 3 qualifications can also give you UCAS points for university.

Where it can lead

A BTEC can lead to:

  • an apprenticeship
  • a job in a related industry
  • another course at a higher level
  • university, if you complete a Level 3 BTEC

Costs and financial support

BTECs are usually free for 16 to 19 year olds in England. If you’re aged 19 or over, you may need to pay a fee depending on your circumstances.

You may be able to get financial support, such as a bursary or Advanced Learner Loan, to help with the cost of your course.
