About diplomas

Diplomas are work-related qualifications that help you gain knowledge and skills for a specific job, industry or subject. You can take a diploma at different levels, depending on your experience and career goals.

Types of diplomas

Diplomas are available at various qualification levels. The most common are:

  • Level 1 and 2 diplomas – equivalent to GCSEs
  • Level 3 diplomas – equivalent to A levels
  • Level 4 and 5 diplomas – equivalent to the first 1 to 2 years of a university degree

Some diplomas are designed to help you enter a specific job or career. Others prepare you to move on to further study or training.

Subjects you can study

Diplomas are available in a wide range of subjects, including:

  • childcare and early years
  • health and social care
  • engineering
  • business and administration
  • creative arts and design
  • hair and beauty
  • construction and trades

Entry requirements

Entry requirements vary depending on the level and subject of the diploma. You may need:

  • no formal qualifications for an entry-level course
  • GCSEs for a Level 2 or 3 diploma
  • previous qualifications or work experience for a higher level diploma

You should check with the course provider for exact entry requirements.
