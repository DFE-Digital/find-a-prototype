Different training options

A Levels

A levels are subject-based qualifications usually assessed by exams. You usually choose 3 subjects to study but you can take more or fewer.

Find out more about A Levels

Apprenticeships

Apprenticeships are real jobs that allow you to earn a wage while you learn. You'll work alongside experienced staff to learn on the job.

Find out more about Apprenticeships

BTECs

BTECs are vocational qualifications that focus on practical, hands-on learning. They can prepare you for work, an apprenticeship or further study, including university.

Find out more about BTECs

Degrees

A degree is a higher education qualification that usually takes 3 or more years to complete if you study full time.

Find out more about Degrees

Diplomas

Diplomas are work-related qualifications that help you gain knowledge and skills for a specific job, industry or subject. You can take a diploma at different levels, depending on your experience and career goals.

Find out more about Diplomas

T Levels

A T Level gives you a mix of learning in the classroom and on the job. Businesses and employers helped design T Levels to teach you the skills you'll need.

Find out more about T Levels
